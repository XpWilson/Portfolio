Sneaky Sneaky

Overview

Sneaky Sneaky was a university project in which I had to master Unityâ€™s built-in Navigation system and learn how to use state machines.

The objective of the game is simple, clear past four short puzzles filled with guards patrolling the area. Once you reach the end you win the game.

The gameplay is rather simple, the player is controlled by a point and click system. The player clicks in the direction they want to go and the avatar follows. The enemies, in this case, the guards follow a predetermined path based on the blue spheres in the area. The Guards all have six states. The patrol state is their default state, which is when all the guards are following their predetermined path. The Idle state is denoted by their sudden darker color palette. This indicates that the Guard has reached one of their patrol spots. They will pause for a few seconds and then proceed to their next location back in the patrol state. The Reverse state is self-explanatory, it reverses the path the Guards follow, the state is active when the Guard appears blue. The pause state is also self-explanatory, the guard is paused mid-state and they will not move until they are unpaused, paused guards appear grey. When the Guards hear the fire alarm has been tampered with they will forget about their previous patrol path and go towards the alarm to deactivate it. This is their Angry state, and once reaching the alarm they will go back to their patrol state. The final state is the Pursue state, this is only activated when the player character gets too close to a Guard. The Guard will chase the player until they are too far out of range.

The bulk of my excitement for this project was learning how to use the Navigation system in Unity. All moving entities including the player are using the NavMesh system to move around the game. Studying AI movements is a topic that fascinated me when first playing open-world games with enemies roaming about. Was it as simple as moving an entity from point A to B or was there more complexity to it? This project was my first taste of that world and I can not wait to use systems like NavMesh more in future projects.
